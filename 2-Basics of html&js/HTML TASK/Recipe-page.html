SIMPLE VADA PAV RECIPE

To make Vada Pav, start by creating a spicy filling with boiled, mashed potatoes and tempered spices, then shape it into balls, dip in gram flour batter, and deep-fry until golden. Next, slice a pav bun and generously apply green chutney and a dry garlic chutney on the inside. Finally, place the hot potato vada inside the pav and serve immediately, often with a side of fried green chillies.

Prepration Time

  • Total : Approximately 45-55 minutes
  • Preparation : 25-30 minutes (Includes boiling potatoes, making the filling, and preparing the batter)
  • Cooking : 20-25 minutes (Includes frying the vadas and chillies)

Ingrediants

  • Potatoes: 4-5 medium, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • Aromatics:1-inch ginger, 3-4 cloves of garlic, 2-3 green chillies (crushed into a coarse paste)
  • Tempering:
    • Oil: 2 teaspoons
    • Mustard seeds (Rai): ½ teaspoon
    • Asafoetida (Hing): A pinch
    • Curry leaves: 8-10
  • Spices: Turmeric powder (Haldi): ¼ teaspoon
  • Herbs: Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped: 2 tablespoons
  • Seasoning:Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling: Create the vada filling by mixing boiled, mashed potatoes with a tempering of mustard seeds, curry leaves, turmeric, and a ginger-garlic-chilli paste.
  2. Shape the Vadas: Once the potato mixture has cooled, divide it into equal portions and shape them into round, slightly flattened patties.
  3. Make the Batter: Whisk gram flour (besan) with salt, spices, and water to create a smooth, thick batter that is free of lumps.
  4. Coat and Fry: Dip each potato vada into the batter, ensuring it's evenly coated, and deep-fry in medium-hot oil until it turns golden brown and crispy.
  5. Prepare the Pav: Slice each pav bun horizontally, leaving one side intact, and generously spread dry garlic chutney and green chutney on the inner surfaces.
  6. Assemble and Serve: Place a hot, freshly fried vada inside the prepared pav, press it gently, and serve immediately with fried green chillies.
